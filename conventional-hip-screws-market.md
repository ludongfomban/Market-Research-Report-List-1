Alloy materials, often comprising stainless steel or cobalt-chromium, offer durability and strength but may have varying levels of biocompatibility. Each material type influences the performance, cost, and clinical outcomes of hip surgeries, shaping preferences among healthcare providers and manufacturers.</p></p>
