<p><ul><li>Turbidity Method</li><li>Colorimetric Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The lipase testing reagent market encompasses various types, notably the turbidity method and the colorimetric method. The turbidity method measures the cloudiness of a sample, indicating lipase activity by evaluating the formation of emulsified fat. In contrast, the colorimetric method utilizes color changes in a reaction mixture to quantify lipase activity, often involving specific substrates that release colorimetric products upon enzyme action. Both methods serve diagnostic purposes, particularly in assessing pancreatic function and diagnosing disorders.</p></p>
