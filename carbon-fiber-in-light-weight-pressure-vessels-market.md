<p><p>The carbon fiber in lightweight pressure vessels market is primarily divided into two types: Polyacrylonitrile (PAN) and Pitch. PAN-based carbon fibers are known for their high tensile strength and durability, making them ideal for high-performance applications. They are widely used in aerospace and automotive sectors. Pitch-based carbon fibers, derived from petroleum or coal tar, offer excellent thermal and electrical conductivity. They are typically utilized in applications requiring high stiffness and thermal resistance, such as industrial and energy storage solutions.</p></p>
