<p><p>Biosensors for point-of-care testing (POCT) encompass various types, each with unique functionalities. Optical sensors utilize light-based detection methods, offering high sensitivity for identifying biomolecules. Electrochemical sensors measure electrical signals produced by chemical reactions, making them ideal for analyzing blood glucose or electrolyte levels. Acoustic sensors, on the other hand, detect changes in sound waves or vibrations caused by biological interactions, providing rapid and accurate results. These technologies enhance diagnostic capabilities, allowing for timely patient management in diverse clinical settings.</p></p>
