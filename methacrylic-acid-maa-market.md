<p><p>The Methacrylic Acid (MAA) market is primarily segmented by production methods: the Acetone Cyanohydrin Method and the Isobutylene Oxidation Method. The Acetone Cyanohydrin Method involves reacting acetone with hydrogen cyanide and sulfuric acid, producing MAA with high purity. Conversely, the Isobutylene Oxidation Method uses isobutylene and oxygen, resulting in a more environmentally friendly process. Both methods cater to different industrial applications, influencing market dynamics, pricing, and the development of advanced techniques in MAA production.</p></p>
