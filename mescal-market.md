<p><p>Mezcal is categorized into three main market types: Mezcal Joven, Mezcal Reposado, and Mezcal Añejo. Mezcal Joven, or "young mezcal," is unaged and showcases the pure flavors of the agave. Mezcal Reposado is aged in wooden barrels for at least two months, allowing it to develop a smoother character with complex flavor notes. Mezcal Añejo is aged for at least one year, resulting in a rich, velvety texture and deeper flavors. Each type appeals to different consumer preferences and occasions.</p></p>
